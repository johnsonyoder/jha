The John Henry Algorithim

Every weekend thousands of civilians place bets on the NFL. The John Henry Algorithim uses 4 factors to make a seemingly random guess an educated one.

  • First Factor

    QBR in Similar Weather Conditions

    The first factor the JHA (John Henry Algorithim) takes into account is the quarterback's rating in similar weather conditions. As statistics will tell, certain quarterbacks play better in early fall than late winter and vice versa. The JHA will utilize the ratings from games withing 15 degrees with similar percipitation patterns in the last 2 seasons. This is a metric that is often overlooked by other models.

  • Second Factor

    The Star Power Factor

    Every player in the NFL is extremely gifted and talented. Teams overall talent levels are closer than most fans/media outlets would like you to believe. With that being said, there are star players in the NFL. The Star Power factor takes into account the force that a team's game-changers will bring to a game.

  • Third Factor

    Home Field Advantage

    The third factor used in the JHA is a common one: Home Field Advantage. Teams in the NFL tend to score 2.8 more points at home than on the road. With an average points per game value of 23.5. There is nearly a 10% benefit coming from playing at home- the JHA takes this into account.

  • Fourth Factor

    The John Henry Factor

    Last but certainly not least comes the John Henry Factor. If you cant figure it out by now you haven't been paying attention. This is the factor that comes along in great teams that simply know how to win!
