The Star Power Factor

Arizona Cardinals

The Cardinals's only star power player, David Johnson, has been on Injured Reserve since Week 1. Their offesnse has struggles to get things working in his absence.
Star Power Score: 0 (2.5 before injury to David Johnson)

The Atlanta Falcons

The Super Bowl losers look to come back with a vengeance. Thei combination of Matt Ryan and Julio Jones remains powerful. Coupled with several all-pros on defense- the Falcons are looking for another Super Bowl run.
Star Power Score: 19 (League Best)

Baltimore Ravens

The Ravens have turned another seemingly average season into a potential playoff birth. Their defense has proven tough and they still have the best kicker in the NFL.
Star Power Score: 2.5

Buffalo Bills

After a dramatic start the Bills look to sneak into the playoffs for the first time in more than a decade. However, as it has been a problem for the Bills for a while now, they do not have any true game changers on the roster.
Star Power Score: 0

Carolina Panthers

After a disatarous 2016 season the Panthers have bounced back well. Their talented roster is looking to show that last year was a fluke and the Panthers are still a Super Bowl team.
Star Power Score: 3.5

Chicago Bears

The Bears are not a playoff team at the moment. However, they have young talent on both sides of the ball and a dynamic young quarterback and running back. Look for the Bears to compete in 2/3 years after adding a few more 1st round picks.
Star Power Score: 0

Cincinnati Bengals

The Bengals, like their division rival Ravens, thrive on a tough defense and are looking to slide into the playoffs with a late season push. Geno Atkins boosts their star power score as he thrives on their strong defensive line.
Star Power Score: 2

Cleveland Browns

The Browns are still the Browns, although they boast a stronger point differential this year as opposed to years passed. Joe Thomas would have given the team a star power score, but he is currently on Injured Reserve.
Star Power Score: 0 (2 before Joe Thomas Injury)

Dallas Cowboys

The Cowboys have fallen back to Earth after a strong 2016 campaign. The loss of Ezekiel Elliot proved important as the Cowboys have struggles since the start of his suspension. They still rank among the top half of teams in the NFL, however.
Star Power Score: 8.5 (10.5 before the suspension of Elliot)

Denver Broncos

It appears to be the beginning of a new era for the not too far removed Super Bowl Champions. There is talent on the Denver roster, but quarterbacking woes prove too big of an obstacle to overcome.
Star Power Score: 6

Detroit Lions

The Lions remain perinial playoff contenders behind Matthew Stafford. The gunslinger has cooled off as of late, but the NFL has leanred not to count the Lions out. Their lack of game-changers remains a problem, however.
Star Power Score: 0

Green Bay Packers

The Packers lost their star power when Aaron Rodgers broke his collarbone against the Vikings. His absence has proved their roster is top heavy and lacking effective stregth throughout.
Star Power Score: 4 (9 before injurgy to Rodgers)

Houston Texans

The Texans lost their 1st round pick, Deshaun Watson, early in the season after a dominant start for the Clemson National Champion. Losses of JJ Watt and Brian Cushing proved to be huge hits for the AFCS defending champions. The Texans are looking to 2018 for hope.
Star Power Score: 3

Indianapolis Colts

The Colts, like the Packers, prove that without their star quartback, Andrew Luck, their roster is nothing to fear. The Colts should be looking to change things up in the 2018 offseason.
Star Power Score: 0

Jacksonville Jaguars

The Jaguars finally seemed to have turned the corner out of the rebuild. Their young talent is extremely promising and quarterback Blake Bortles has proved sufficent enough. The offseason addition of Calais Campbell has proved important.
Star Power Score: 6

Kansas City Chiefs

After a strong start the Chiefs have confused the NFL by taking questionable losses to both New York teams. There is talent on the Chiefs roster and Andy Reid has cemented himself as a top coach in the NFL. No one knows where this Chiefs team goes from here.
Star Power Score: 9.5

Los Angeles Chargers

The Chargers are beginning to show the talent they have on their roster. A strong defensive force may be enough to bring the Chargers in the playoffs fromt the weak AFCW.
Star Power Score: 4

Los Angeles Rams

The LA Rams have been the susprise of the 2017 NFL season. After the hire of 31 year old, Sean Mcvay, the Rams now have the most potent offense in the NFL even without any true game-changers on offense.
Star Power Score: 3.5

Miami Dolphins

After an impressive John Henry Factor last season, the Dolphins have managed to squeeze out a few wins this season. Their roster is relativley weak and some questionable decisions from the front office have left Coach Adam Gase with few tools to work with.
Star Power Score: 4

Minnesota Vikings

Some would say the Vikings are the best team in the NFL this season. They are currently the #1 team in the NFC and have proved they can beat any team in the NFL. Vikings fans are holding their breath, quickly thinking (with a lot of others) that this could finally be the year.
Star Power Score: 7

New England Patriots

The Patriots are still the Patriots and before either Belichick or Tom Brady retire it seems they will continue to be the super power they are in the NFL. It would be a mistake to not look at the Patriots as Super Bowl contenders, especially with the additions of Brandin Cooks and Stephen Gilmore.
Star Power Score: 16

New Orleans Saints

The Saints are looking to make a Super Bowl run of their own as rookies Alvin Kamara and Marshon Lattimore look to share offensive and defensive rookie of the year titles. The NFL will wait to see if Drew Brees has one more Super Bowl left in him.
Star Power Score: 5

New York Giants

It's hard to recall a team that has under-preformed more than the 2017 Giants. Once a pre-season Super Bowl hopeful, the Giants have self-destructed behind the loss of star reciever, Odell Beckham Jr. The Giants need a big 2018 offense to re-allign their offensive and defensive talents.
Star Power Score: 6 (14 before injuries to Beckham Jr. and Jenkins)

New York Jets

Once thought to be the worst team in the NFL, the Jets have suprised the league with a few quality wins so far this season. However, their roster is painfully devoid of talent, much less star power.
Star Power Score: 0

Oakland Raiders

Last year's John Henry Factor top rated team, the Raiders have had good games and bad games so far into 2017. They have a moderatley talented roster and, by the claim of this website, a nose for winning. These type of teams can not discounted come playoff time.

Philadelphia Eagles

The Eagles have proved their talented roster and young quarterback are enough to give them a playoff birth, very potentially a first round bye. After a strong 2018 offseason, it is hard to find the weak point in the Eagles roster or playstyle.

Pittsburg Steelers

The Steelers remain an AFC powerhouse and will once again look to make a Super Bowl run behind league leading reciever Antonio Brown. Of course, their starch defense and legendary Big Ben anything is possible for the Steelers this season. The addition of a healthy, un-suspended Leveon Bell doesn't hurt their chances.

San Fransisco 49ers

The 49ers are still in rebuilding mode, but the recent addition of quarterback Jimmy Garrapolo may mark a turning point for the 49ers. The bay area front office must look to develop their young talent and add more talent and potenial game-changers as their current roster simply lacks star power.
Star Power Score: 0

Seattle Seahawks

The Seahawks have suffered a few significant injuries in their 2017 quest for another Super Bowl. Despite this fact, the Seashawks look poised for a deep playoff run. Russell Wilson has again proven he is an elite quarterback, but after losing several pro-bowlers Seattles resilience will be tested.

Tampa Bay Buccaneers

There is plently of talent on the Buccaneers roster which leads one to ask in head coach Dick Koetter is responsible for the team's lackluster performance.

Tennessee Titans

The Titans sit at the top of the AFCS with wins over Jacksonville and Seattle. There are few to zero real game-changing players for the Titans, but the run heavy attack and stout Dick Lebeau defense has proved it has what it takes to win games. The Titans should have a high John Henry Factor at the end of the season.

Washington Redskins

The Kirk Cousin's lead Redskins are still competeing for a spot in the playoffs, but have once again looked to be one of the most run-of-the-mill teams in the NFL. The Redskins will look to keep Cousins over the offseason and add a few more pieces to improve the overall talent level of their roster.
